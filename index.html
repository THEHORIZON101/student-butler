          What paid plans would actually fund

          Orbit can be useful as a local planning tool today. Paid plans would support real accounts, reliable saved history across devices, deeper review tools, future AI extraction, and future image/PDF support. Those features require backend infrastructure and are not faked here.

          FAQ

          Clear answers before launch.

          Orbit helps students know what to do first without pretending this static site is a full backend product.

          Does this demo use real AI?

          No. The demo uses transparent frontend JavaScript rules. A production version could add real AI later with a backend.

          Does Orbit upload my schoolwork?

          No. This GitHub Pages version runs in your browser. The saved Orbit Card uses localStorage on your device only.

          Can it read worksheets, images, or PDFs?

          Not in this static demo. Image/PDF support is listed as a future Pro feature because it would require additional infrastructure.

          Are payments active?

          No. Pricing buttons only open a coming-soon modal. They do not collect payment, credit cards, passwords, or account information.

          Why use Orbit instead of a normal to-do list?

          A to-do list stores tasks. Orbit turns the pile into a sequence: what matters first, how long it may take, and how to start.
